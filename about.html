A musical chameleon, Vera Marie Weber is a music producer, film-composer, singer, and classical pianist, specialized in contemporary piano and Baroque harpsichord performance. Vera is currently finishing her Bachelor’s degree in Music in the Performer-Composer program at California Institute of the Arts. She studies piano performance with Vicki Ray, harpsichord with Patricia Mabee, and composition with Nicholas Deyoe.

Vera has been playing piano since age 5, and it has been her main focus for the majority of her musical career. She started as a piano performance major in college, and regularly performs in prestigious LA venues such as the Roy and Edna Disney CalArts Theater (REDCAT) at Walt Disney Concert Hall, Colburn’s Zipper Hall, Artshare LA, and more. She played the world premier of a John Cage piece at REDCAT in 2016. In 2018, Vera recorded the debut of a piano solo by Eve Beglarian, which she also performed at REDCAT. She was a harpsichord soloist in Schnittke’s Concerto Grosso alongside renowned violinists Andrew McIntosh and Lorenz Gamma in 2017 with “The Ensemble” at CalArts.

Vera has composed music for over 12 films, including both feature and short films, with genres ranging from drama, thriller, comedy, indie, horror, children’s film, and documentary. Her film scores have been shown and recognized at REDCAT, San Diego Asian Film Festival, Newport Beach Film Festival, Queens Film Festival, Indian Cine Festival, Asheville Film Festival, Hungary Mediawave Festival, and more. In 2016, Vera was nominated for “best film score” at the Revolution Me Film Festival in Brooklyn, New York for her work in Thirst for Life. Since age 16, Vera has been composer assistant for her father and mentor Matthias Weber, an academy award winning film composer in Germany and Austria. Vera regularly works as a studio musician playing piano, harpsichord, mandolin, and voice.

Vera sings, writes, produces, and plays mandolin and zither in the music group Primero Sueño, a feminist world-folk band based in Los Angeles. Vera went on tour with Primero Sueño in Mexico in 2017, and has performed and recorded in venues such as The Troubadour, Capitol Records, LA Plaza de Cultura y Artes, and the USC Fisher Museum. Primero Sueño was honored to perform for the world premier of a Frida Kahlo exhibit at the Museum of Latin American Art in Long Beach. In 2018, Vera was the producer for Primero Sueño’s debut album Ecos de Mi Raiz. Vera sings in German, Spanish, and English.

Before college, Vera was a consistent winner of prestigious piano competitions in the Music Teachers Association of California. She received both Panel Honors and 1st Place at the Memorial Awards, and was regularly selected for Bach Master classes. In 2014, Vera played a contemporary piano concerto by James Domine with the San Fernando Valley Symphony at the Thousand Oaks Civic Arts Plaza.

Vera started her vocal training at Calabasas High School when she was part of the acclaimed a cappella group “Bare Rhythm.” With this group she performed at Carnegie Hall and went on tour in China in 2015.

Vera has 6 years of experience being a piano teacher, and additionally gives lessons in voice, mandolin, composition, and music production.
